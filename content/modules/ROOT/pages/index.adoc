This hands-on demo shows **Ansible Automation Platform (AAP) 2.6** with **EnterpriseDB (EDB) Postgres Advanced Server** in an active/passive, multi-datacenter disaster recovery architecture. You will see primary workload in DC1, a streaming replica in DC2, and a live failover that activates the standby AAP instance.

toc::[]

== Demo intent

* Show customers how **AAP + EDB Postgres on RHEL** delivers enterprise-grade automation with DR capability -- **RTO under 5 minutes, RPO under 5 seconds**.
* Demonstrate a controlled failover: stop the primary database, promote the replica, and bring up the standby AAP -- all within the lab.

== What you will see

* **DC1 (active)** -- `{aap_dc1_host}` running AAP, connected to `{pg_dc1_host}` (EDB Postgres primary)
* **DC2 (passive)** -- `{aap_dc2_host}` with AAP services stopped, connected to `{pg_dc2_host}` (EDB Postgres standby)
* **Streaming replication** -- asynchronous replication from DC1 to DC2, continuously copying all AAP data
* **Failover** -- in Module 4, you will trigger a DR event and see DC2 come online
